# Raj Avinsys — Product Walkthrough Guide

**A 20-stage tour of CRMOS through the eyes of your team.**

This guide walks you through the entire product, one role at a time, following the natural flow of an EPC project from first enquiry to ongoing service. Each stage shows you which member of your team would be doing the work, what they see, and what they do next.

---

## How to use this guide

1. Open **https://localhost:3000/demo** in your browser (or whichever URL we shared with you).
2. You'll see six cards — one for each team role. Pick the persona at the start of each stage.
3. Click "Sign in as …" — you'll land directly on that person's daily workspace.
4. Follow the click-by-click steps in each act.

> Same password for all roles: **`RajDemo@1234`**
> To switch roles mid-flow, click your avatar in the top-right → "Sign out" → return to `/demo`.

# Part 1 — Winning the work

## Act 1 — A new opportunity lands

**👤 Sign in as Anil**

The story starts at a GIDC industry meet where Anil bumps into the Plant Head of Vapi Specialty Chemicals. They're planning a major expansion and need electrical contracting work.

1. You'll land on the **Field** workspace — Anil's home base.
2. You'll see today's attendance (he's already checked in) and his planned visits.
3. Click **Leads** in the left sidebar.
4. You'll see one fresh lead: **"Vapi Specialty Chemicals — Panel + EPC enquiry"** — captured yesterday.
5. Click into the lead to see the details:
   - Contact name + phone (raw, captured at the event)
   - Estimated project value: ₹85 lakh
   - Expected close: in 45 days
   - Notes from the conversation

> **What this represents in your business:** Every casual conversation that could turn into business is now tracked from day one. No more "remind me — who was that person at the trade show last month?"

## Act 3 — Lead becomes a Project

**👤 Sign out, then sign in as Rakesh**

The next morning, Anil hands over the qualified lead to Rakesh. The customer is serious; it's time to formalise the opportunity as a Project.

1. You'll land on the **Projects** workspace.
2. You'll see Rakesh's active projects — including the 4 from quotes already won.
3. To create a new project from the qualified lead, you would:
   - Go back to **Leads**
   - Open the Vapi lead
   - Click "Convert to project" (this would create a new project automatically inheriting the lead's customer + value)
4. For now, click into one of the existing projects (e.g. **"Adani Hazira – EPC"** or similar).
5. You'll see the **Project Detail** page with these tabs:
   - **Overview** — stakeholders, scannable progress, key dates
   - **Specifications** — what the customer needs
   - **Samples** — drawings and product samples
   - **Quotes** — quotations sent
   - **Orders** — sales orders raised
   - **Documents** — attachments
   - **Timeline** — every change, audit-ready

> **What this represents in your business:** A project gets a single home. Everyone — sales, projects, accounts, service — sees the same picture. No more "let me check with finance" or "let me look in another file."

## Act 4 — Building the quote

**👤 Stay signed in as Rakesh**

The customer's specification is locked. It's time to price the work.

1. Inside the project, click the **Quotes** tab.
2. You'll see quotes already raised against this project.
3. Click into any quote to see:
   - Line-by-line pricing (cables, panels, labour, freight)
   - Subtotal, GST split (CGST + SGST or IGST), grand total
   - Customer terms
   - Margin chip (visible to managers, masked from junior staff)
4. Click **"Print BOQ"** to see the PDF version Rakesh would share with the customer.
5. Click **"Share via WhatsApp"** to see how the quote goes out.

> **What this represents in your business:** Pricing consistency. Every quote follows the same format, uses the same master pricing, applies the same GST rules. Margin protection is built-in.

**👤 Sign out, then sign in as Rakesh**

## Act 7 — Mehul fields the PR + sends RFQ

**👤 Sign out, then sign in as Mehul**

Mehul receives Rakesh's PR. For high-value items, he compares quotes from multiple vendors.

1. You'll land on the **Procurement** workspace.

6. Click **+ New RFQ** to see the form:
   - Source PR (link back)

## Act 8 — Comparative Statement → Pick the winner

**👤 Stay signed in as Mehul**

Once vendors respond with their quotes, Mehul can compare them side-by-side.

1. Inside any RFQ with responses, click **"Open CS"** (Comparative Statement).
2. You'll see a vendor × line matrix:
   - Each cell = that vendor's quoted total for that line
   - **L1 (lowest) auto-highlighted in amber**
   - Click any cell to select that vendor for that line
   - If you pick non-L1, the system asks for an override reason
3. Click **"Finalise CS"** when done.
4. From the CS, click **"Create PO from CS"** to raise the actual purchase order.

> **What this represents in your business:** L1 is no longer an excuse for poor decisions. You can pick the best vendor (even if not cheapest) with documented justification. Procurement becomes defensible — every rupee spent has a paper trail.

# Part 4 — Paying the vendor

## Act 13 — Booking the vendor's bill

**👤 Sign out, then sign in as Priya**

You'll land on the **Vendor Bills** screen — Priya's daily workspace. The vendor has couriered their tax invoice; she books it.

1. You'll see 4 bills across various states.
2. **Critical:** Look at **RA-VB-2026-0002** — it's marked **"mismatched"** (yellow/amber).
3. Click into it to see the 3-way match diagnostic:
   - **Bill says:** 1 unit × ₹1,85,000 = ₹1,85,000
   - **PO said:** 1 unit × ₹1,75,000 = ₹1,75,000
   - **Variance:** +₹10,000 (5.7% rate mismatch)
4. The system flagged this **before** Priya approved the bill.
5. Priya now has options:
   - Reject the bill back to the vendor (their invoice is wrong)
   - Amend the PO (the rate genuinely changed)
   - Approve with override (and the reason gets logged forever)

> **What this represents in your business:** Vendors no longer get to "test" you with higher rates on invoices. Every rate, every GST percent, every HSN code is checked automatically.

## Act 14 — Paying the vendor with TDS

**👤 Stay signed in as Priya**

The bill is approved. Time to pay.

1. From the bill detail, click **"Pay vendor"** (sky-blue CTA).
2. You'll see the payment form:
   - Gross amount auto-filled
   - **TDS section auto-suggested** based on vendor type:
     - Polycab (supplier) → §194Q @ 0.1%
     - A contractor would route to §194C @ 1%
     - A consultant would route to §194J @ 10%
   - PAN check — if PAN is missing, §206AA penalty rate (20%) applies automatically
   - **Net amount = gross − TDS** (computed live)
3. Choose payment mode (NEFT / RTGS / cheque / UPI / cash).
4. Click **"Post payment"** — the bill flips to paid, TDS sits in a holding state for the 7th-of-next-month deposit deadline.

5. From the payment detail, click **"Print voucher"** to get the PDF receipt.
6. Click **"Form 16A"** for the TDS certificate (used at year-end for the vendor).

> **What this represents in your business:** TDS becomes automatic — not a quarterly panic. Every payment is GST-and-TDS-compliant from the moment it's posted.

# Part 5 — Tax compliance

## Act 16 — Monthly GSTR-2B reconciliation

**👤 Stay signed in as Priya**

Around the 14th of every month, the government publishes your GSTR-2B — a statement listing every invoice your vendors reported supplying to you.

1. Click **Procurement → GSTR-2B**.
2. Click **"Upload GSTR-2B"** to upload the CSV downloaded from the GSTN portal.
3. The system reconciles vendor-by-vendor:
   - **Matched** — invoice exists in books AND in 2B (✓ ITC eligible)
   - **In books not in 2B** — vendor forgot to file (call them!)
   - **In 2B not in books** — vendor reported something we didn't book (investigate)
   - **Amount mismatch** — different ₹ amounts (resolve before claiming ITC)
4. The dashboard shows you the financial impact: how much ITC is at risk if mismatches aren't resolved.

> **What this represents in your business:** GST input credit no longer leaks. Every rupee of GST paid is matched to a government-confirmed entry. Annual savings at scale: lakhs of rupees.

## Act 17 — AP Ageing + MSME compliance

**👤 Stay signed in as Priya**

1. Click **Procurement → AP Ageing**.
2. You'll see the cash-out dashboard:
   - 5-bucket ageing (Current, 1-30, 31-60, 61-90, 90+)
   - Click any bucket to filter the bills
   - **MSME card** showing breach + warning vendors
3. The MSME 45-day rule kicks in: if any MSME vendor is owed for > 45 days, you owe 3× bank-rate interest. The dashboard flags them so you can prioritise.
4. Click **"Export MSME-1"** to download the half-yearly filing CSV (if there are breaches).

> **What this represents in your business:** Cash-out planning becomes proactive. MSME compliance becomes automatic (saving you penal interest + audit hassles).
